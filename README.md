# Marathon Countdown

A small marathon **orientation** tool. Not a coach, not a training planner.

You enter your race date and, if you like, your current weekly volume. The tool
then shows, for right now:

- a clear countdown ("X weeks until race day"),
- which phase you are roughly in, or that you are still in open base phase before
  any structured plan would begin,
- how the major published marathon plans would treat this same point in time,
  side by side (Higdon, Pfitzinger, Hansons),
- where your own weekly volume sits against that spread.

The prescriptive apps answer "what do I do today?" This one answers "where should
I roughly be right now, and how do the major philosophies disagree at this point?"

## What it is not

- Not a day by day workout plan. For the real schedules, follow the source link
  on each plan and go to Higdon's site, the Pfitzinger book, or Hansons.
- Not a replacement for those plans. It characterizes and compares them and sends
  you to the real thing.
- Not a beginner habit builder. It assumes you already run.

## The plans

Five well known builds, from accessible to high volume: Hal Higdon Novice 1,
Intermediate 1 and Advanced 1, the Hansons Marathon Method, and Pfitzinger 18/70.
Each is shown as a short characterization with a link to its source.

The volume numbers you see are **approximate**. They are generated by this tool
from a few shape parameters and snapped to the nearest 5 km on purpose. They are
enough to orient and to choose a plan, never enough to reconstruct a plan's real
week by week schedule. Every plan links out to its own source, which is where the
real schedule lives.

## Privacy

Everything runs in your browser. Your settings (race date, weekly volume, block
length) are saved in this browser's local storage and are never sent anywhere.
There are no accounts, no cookies, no analytics, and no network requests while the
tool runs.

Local storage is readable by other software on your device and can be cleared by
the browser (some browsers evict it after a period of disuse). Use **Export** to
save a small JSON backup of your settings, and **Import** to restore it.

## Running it

It is a static site: plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, no build step and no
dependencies. It is meant for GitHub Pages.

The scripts are ES modules, so opening `index.html` straight from the file system
will not work in most browsers. To try it locally, serve the folder over HTTP, for
example:

```bash
python3 -m http.server
```

then open the address it prints. On GitHub Pages it just works: set Pages to serve
from the branch, and the site is live at its Pages URL.

## Files

- `index.html` - the page shell.
- `styles.css` - all styling.
- `plans.js` - the plan characterizations and shape parameters (edit plans here).
- `engine.js` - pure functions: the curves, phases, and comparison logic.
- `storage.js` - load and save settings, export and import.
- `app.js` - rendering and interaction.
- `icon.png` - the app icon.
